No current governance model was designed for the future of learning. Now what?

Parent dissatisfaction is rising. The federal role in education is shifting. Accountability systems are fracturing. The result is unprecedented uncertainty and, potentially, a design moment for fundamental change.

Nine principles for future-ready governance

Fourteen of the nation's leading experts in education governance and the future of learning spent four months asking a question no existing system was built to answer: How do we govern a learning ecosystem that actually helps every young person flourish in life, career, and democracy in the intelligence age?

They identified nine principles that future-ready governance should embody:

Meaningful learner and family agency
Equitable, portable funding
A diverse provider ecosystem
Adaptive, performance-based accountability
Learner data as a personal asset
Multiple pathways to mastery
Individual thriving and the common good
Governance that adapts its own rules
A guaranteed safety net for every learner

The Process

LearnerStudio convened the H3 Governance Design Group in 2025 to tackle a challenge no existing governance model was designed for: how to govern a learning ecosystem that helps every young person flourish in the intelligence age.

The group was selected for its diversity of perspective, spanning the political spectrum from progressive community organizers to market-oriented reformers, from state policy experts to school founders. What everyone shared was dissatisfaction with the status quo and commitment to learner-centered goals.

Over four sessions spanning August through October 2025, the group mapped the current governance landscape, analyzed alignment with the future-ready learning vision, forged governance design principles through intensive debate, and began identifying fertile ground for experimentation. The process modeled what the group believes future-ready governance requires: the capacity to hold productive tension and disagree without disengaging.
